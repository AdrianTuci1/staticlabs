# OUR MISSION

### RESEARCH FOR BETTER HUMAN-MACHINE COLLABORATION
Static Labs is an AI and machine learning research laboratory focused on improving the way people work with computational systems. We study the interaction layer between human intent and machine execution, with an emphasis on interfaces that make complex workflows faster, clearer, and more accountable.

Our work combines applied model research, product engineering, and interaction design. The goal is not to add automation for its own sake, but to build systems that help people make better decisions, complete demanding work with less friction, and understand how AI is shaping the result.

#### RESEARCH PRINCIPLES
1. **HUMAN CONTROL BY DESIGN**: AI systems should accelerate expert work while keeping review, correction, and responsibility visible.
2. **LOW-LATENCY INTELLIGENCE**: Better interaction depends on speed, but speed is only useful when precision and reliability are preserved.
3. **WORKFLOW-NATIVE MODELS**: Machine learning should adapt to the context of real professional workflows, not force teams to reorganize around model limitations.
4. **OBSERVABLE AUTOMATION**: Autonomous systems must expose their assumptions, confidence, and recommended actions in language humans can inspect.

#### ACTIVE RESEARCH TRACKS
**Thunder** investigates diffusion-based language modeling for conversation and short code completion. The model refines blocks of text simultaneously and pairs that approach with kernel-level optimization for ultra-fast interactive use.

**Statsparrot** is an autonomous no-ETL data engineering system. Teams connect data sources and receive insights, signal interpretation, and recommended decisions without maintaining brittle transformation pipelines.

**Pixtooth** applies machine learning to dental charting. The system supports clinical documentation for dental practices and explores auto-charting workflows that remain clinician-reviewed, traceable, and practical in daily care.

#### THE LAB
Static Labs builds research prototypes where model behavior, interface quality, and operational value are evaluated together. We publish our work as systems: concrete artifacts that can be tested, used, and improved in the environments they are meant to serve.
